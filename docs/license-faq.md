It depends. Internal evaluation, learning, or non-commercial research may be allowed, but commercial product use is not included in the public license. Ask before using RepoMori in a business workflow that supports a paid product or service.

To discuss commercial licensing, contact the COO of TWO HANDS NETWORK LTD.

## Can I Fork It?

GitHub may allow viewing and forking public repositories, but the license still controls what you may do with the software. Forks must keep the required notices and remain within the non-commercial license unless a separate written license is granted.

## Can I Submit Pull Requests?

Yes, but read [CONTRIBUTING.md](../CONTRIBUTING.md) first. Contributions must be compatible with RepoMori's licensing and may be used by TWO HANDS NETWORK LTD under separate commercial licenses.

## Why Not MIT Or Apache?

Those licenses are permissive and allow commercial product use. RepoMori's current goal is to allow personal and non-commercial use while reserving commercial product rights.

No. This FAQ explains the project's intended permission boundaries. Get legal advice before relying on it for enforcement, investment, acquisition, or commercial licensing decisions.
